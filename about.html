About

Two people, deeply connected.

Rim & Otter began between two nature- and animal-loving friends — one who has spent over fifteen years holding sacred space for others, and one who kept asking questions, fascinated by the non-ordinary.

The founders

Kristy Sweetland

Co-founder · Nature-spirit work · Teaching

Rooted in New Mexico: Traditional land of the Pueblos, Ute, and Tiwa people.

Kristy has been helping people develop their deep connection with nature for nearly two decades. With a bachelor's degree in organizational psychology and a master's degree in transpersonal psychology, she works with individuals via her private practice, Coaching to Come Alive. Kristy is a credentialed Certified Professional Transformational Coach and a Board-Certified Executive Coach. She holds professional certificates in neuro-transformation and resilience psychology, too.

After a life-changing, spontaneous shamanic initiation (read about it in her award-winning book, Stark Raving Zen: A Memoir of Coming Alive), Kristy studied with multiple shamanic teachers, the most pivotal Alberto Villoldo, who also teaches channeled light energy.

Shari Vaccaro

Co-founder · Strategy · Communications

Rooted in Minnesota: Traditional land of the Wahpekute and Ochethi Sakowin people.

Shari is an empath and lifelong, existential ponderer who was raised with an appreciation for both animals and the outdoors. She has a bachelor's degree in studio art and years of writing experience. For the last 10+ years, she's been strategizing brand communications and building experiences that help people with their health. Bringing Rim & Otter into existence is a big step in her own search for meaning.

Shari is a thinking partner, a calm, grounding presence who people have always turned to, in every room she's been in professionally and personally.

The founding story

How we came together, and why now.

A mutual love of animals initially brought Kristy Sweetland and Shari Vaccaro together in their youth, when they worked at an animal hospital in Minnesota.

Connected on a soul level and with unwavering belief in each other, they've navigated decades of individual transformation. And, no surprise, each has always felt most like themselves outside in nature, especially together and with their spouses. They'd each separately come to a point where they ached to do more — for Kristy, expanding her work in animism, and Shari, a heart project that included animals. Neither was sure how.

One day, in April of 2026, while chatting, they agreed out loud to open a little door to the possibility of doing something together — and bam. The door flew open with plentiful, undeniable ally signs. All the stars started lining up for this partnership, and Rim & Otter was born — right on time.

Why the name Rim & Otter?

Rim: A rim is an ancient, sacred landform, the edge between worlds, the threshold, the place where perspective changes entirely. You can see everything from a rim. It’s where the ordinary view ends and something larger begins. It was chosen with a reverence for New Mexico.

Otter: The river otter represents emotional freedom, curiosity, balance, and community. Importantly, their return from near extinction signifies ecosystem renewal and resilience. The DNR views them as an “indicator species” for healthy waters. It was chosen for teaching interdependence in the North Woods of Minnesota.

&: The ampersand connects two completely different relational forces, a reminder that everything has a spirit you were born to know.
